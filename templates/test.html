Cereal crops like rice, wheat, maize, sorghum and millets etc. are the principal components of human diet and important staples for daily survival of majority of the people across the globe. These crops belong to poaceae family and are rich in vitamins, minerals and fiber. Cereal crops are adversely affected by abiotic stresses like cold, drought, heat and salinity. With the advent of modern NGS technologies, the plethora of molecular data leads to infer many unexplored facts of the cereal crops using in-silico approaches. Functional protein classification is one of the challenging tasks, involving higher cost and time. DeepAProt is a deep learning-based web server for the classification of abiotic stress functional protein sequences of poaceae crops using their protein sequences in fasta format. The different features considered while developing the machine-learning based models were amino acid composition, secondary protein structures (alpha-helix, beta-sheet, and coil), stability, etc. which were extracted using the bio-python package. This web-based prediction tool will assist the researchers for rapid and cost limited prediction of cold, heat, drought, and salt stress associated functional protein sequence from crops belonging to Poaceae family.
